Understanding Principal Components

What are Principal Components?

Each PC (Principal Component) is a weighted combination of all measured biomarkers (blood tests, physical measurements, etc.). Through SVD/PCA analysis, the algorithm identified patterns in how biomarkers vary together across the NHANES population.

Color Coding:

Bars are color-coded by the biological system each PC primarily represents (as shown in the legend). These assignments are based on the dominant biomarker loadings in each principal component.

PC Values:

  • Positive values: Your biomarker pattern in this PC is above the reference population average
  • Negative values: Your biomarker pattern is below the reference population average
  • Magnitude: How far you deviate from the reference (values typically range -5 to +5)

Selected PCs:

The model uses 17 sex-specific PCs (not PC1-17 sequentially, but specific PCs like PC1, PC2, PC5, PC6, etc.). These were chosen because they best predict mortality risk (biological aging) in Cox proportional hazards models.

Interpretation:

Each PC captures different biological patterns:

  • PC1: Usually captures the largest variance (often overall health/aging)
  • PC2: Second-largest independent pattern
  • Higher PCs: More specific biological systems (inflammation, metabolism, cardiovascular, kidney function, etc.)

Your Biological Age:

The Cox model weighs all these PCs together to estimate your mortality risk, which is converted to biological age. The specific contribution of each PC to your final age is determined by the model coefficients.
